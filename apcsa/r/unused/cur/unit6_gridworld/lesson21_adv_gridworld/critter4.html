The case study goes on to describe two classes that extend Critter: ChameleonCritter and CrabCritter.

  • A ChameleonCritter gathers the same actors as a Critter does. Instead of eating them, however, it chooses a random actor from the list and changes its own color to match that of the chosen actor. It moves like a Critter, except that it also turns toward the new location as it moves.
  • A CrabCritter gathers actors that are located either immediately in front of it, immediately to its left-front, or immediately to its right-front. If those actors aren't Rocks or Critters, it eats them. It then moves randomly to the right or left, unless both locations are blocked, in which case it turns 90° randomly to the left or right.
