In sound recording, noise is something to be avoided. In some recordings you can hear a background "hiss" that was introduced by the microphones or other equipment. In this exercise, though, let's add some noise to a recording.

Noise is, essentially, randomness. When a samples in a sound jump around randomly rather than following a waveform, that means that noise will be heard. If the random jumping is large, the noise will be louder.

Write a method addNoise which takes a single numeric argument. This method will add noise to every invdividual sample in the sound, with the amount of noise based on the argument.

By including an argument in this method, you can decide how much noise you want to add each time you call it. What is the best form the argument should take? That is, should the argument require a double or an int? And, what is the range of the number that the argument expects? Many different values can work. The important thing is to think about what the minimum and maximum noise you might want to add is.

Try adding noise to one of the sounds of voices, and see how much noise you can add and still make out the words. Be sure to get a sense of what noise looks like in the visualizer.
