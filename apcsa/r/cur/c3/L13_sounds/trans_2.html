Obviously just spreading all the samples in a sound out can be problematic. What professionals will often do is normalize a sound, which means making it as loud as possible without changing its character.

In order to normalize, you need to:

  • figure out what the largest individual sample is by taking the absolute value of every sample -- you are trying to find the sample whose value is farthest from zero whether that be a maximum or minimum.
  • find the factor by which you multiply the maximum from the last step to reach 32,767
  • multiply every number in the sample by your factor from the previous step

Write the method normalize, which takes no arguments and normalizes the sound.

Two hints:

  1. You will need two loops, one after the other.
  2. You can find the factor you need by using ratios: your factor is equal to the maximum possible integer value(32,767) divided by the maximum number in your sample.
