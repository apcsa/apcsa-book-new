Describe your choice of argument

You gave the addNoise method a single numeric argument. Describe the type and other characteristics of the argument. Why did you choose to make it that way?

I chose to make my argument a double, and accepted a range between 0 and 1. 0 means no noise, and 1 means complete and total noise! I used a little math to convert that to a positive or negative number between (at most) -32000 and +32000.
I chose to make addNoise take an int, between -32768 and 32767. Then, I made a random number bwebetween 0 and that argument. But, it didn't work quite right, because I didn't get both negative and positive noise! So, I had to get another random number, either -1 or +1, and then I multiplied by that. The hardest part was making sure the individual signal didn't get too big or too small.
