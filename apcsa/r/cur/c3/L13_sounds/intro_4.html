No. It is not the size of individual samples that determine the volume of a sound, but the size of the difference between the biggest and smallest samples.

No, change in pitch and volume only come through changes in the individual samples.

Correct. When there is no change in pressure, there is no sound.

Not at all. When you have a sound sampled at sufficient quality (and a sampling rate of 22,050 samples per second is of sufficient quality), you will hear exactly what the sound is. Said another way, an ArrayList of numbers like this will determine a sound exactly.
