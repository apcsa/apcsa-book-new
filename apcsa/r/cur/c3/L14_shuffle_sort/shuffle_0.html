We will be using a Deck class whose instance variables include myCards, an ArrayList<Card>. We start by initializing this deck.

The Deck constructor calls the method reset(). The method reset() should initialize the deck to be in perfect order:

  • Initialize the myCards arraylist: myCards = new ArrayList();
  • The top thirteen cards are the clubs, arranged from ace to king.
  • The next thirteen cards are the diamonds, arranged in the same way.
  • Next come the hearts, and finally the spades, arranged from ace to king.

Note: Look at the toString() method in the Card class. It shows what integer values are mapped to what suits and ranks. It is zero-based as you might imagine. For ranks, 0 to 12 maps from Ace to King; for suits 0 to 3 maps to clubs, diamonds, hearts, and spades.
