Here is the pseudocode from before, translated to work with a deck of cards.

  1. Initialize sortedSoFar to empty.
  2. If myCards is nonempty, perform the sorting.
    1. Choose the lowest-ranking card in myCards.
    2. Remove it from myCards and add it to the end of sortedSoFar.
    3. Repeat this process until no more elements remain in myCards.

Suppose that the deck contains five cards, and consider the contents of myCards and sortedSoFar immediately after the second execution of step 2b. If the code is working correctly, several logical properties will be true at this point:

  • sortedSoFar contains two cards, and myCards contains three cards.
  • The cards in sortedSoFar are arranged in order from lowest-ranking to highest-ranking.
  • All the cards in the original myCards are in either the current myCards or in sortedSoFar, and all the cards in sortedSoFar and myCards are in the original myCards.

Give another property, different from those just listed, that relates the contents of sortedSoFar and the current myCards, and will be true if the algorithm is running correctly.

There aren't any cards that are in both sortedSoFar and myCards.
All the cards in sortedSoFar are lower-ranking than any of the cards in myCards.
