How good is the "perfect" shuffle? To analyze its behavior, let's assume that we have a deck of four cards, A, B, C, and D. We split the deck in half: A B and C D. Then we interleave the two halves to get A C B D. Doing this one more time get us back to the original deck!

Perhaps it works better with a bigger deck? Trying a deck of size 8 results in equally disappointing behavior:
After the first shuffle: A E B F C G D H
After the second shuffle: A C E G B D F H
After the third shuffle: A B C D E F G H

We conclude that a perfect shuffle is too predictable to satisfy the problem specifications, so we move on to alternative solutions.
