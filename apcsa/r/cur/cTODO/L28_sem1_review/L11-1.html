The Monty Hall simulation will not be covered on your AP exam. However, you will be expected to be able to read and understand large programs.

To review this skill, go back to your Monty Hall program in BlueJ. Instead of working with the code directly from BlueJ, try printing it out and reading it on paper.

See if you can still understand the way the program works. If not, you should practice this skill with other projects from the course as well.

Remember: you won't have a computer to work with during the AP exam!
