The "Turing Test" refers to a contest to tell the difference between a human and a computer pretending to be human. The test involves text-only responses to written questions. For example: imagine receiving a series of email replies from "Stranger A" and "Stranger B" knowing one of them was actually a computer imitating a human.

The Turing Test examines a computer's ability to demonstrate "artificial" (human-like) intelligence. Or, really, how good programmers are at writing a computer program that can read messages and write convincing replies.
