In some ways, you can think of this course window like a course guidebook, where you'll spend portions of your time reading about programming issues.

Reading pages are used to:

  • Introduce new concepts. If a concept is particularly important, we'll highlight it in a key concept box over to the right (see icon).
  • Explain a new wrinkle, or detail, to a concept that you've been working with for a while.
  • Define a new vocabulary word. New terms, like recursion, will be highlighted and appear as a dynamic link in a vocabulary box
    (see icon) to the right. Click any vocabulary link in the box to see its definition in the Course Glossary.


We'll keep these reading pages as short as possible. But keep in mind that they generally contain important information, so don't skip them just to jump ahead to the assignments.

Key points will be summarized out here in the right margin, highlighted with a special Key icon.
