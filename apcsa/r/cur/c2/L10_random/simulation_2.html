Consider the following game, referred to as the St. Petersburg Lottery.

  1. I pay a fixed entry fee, and a pot is initialized to $1.
  2. A fair coin is flipped repeatedly. If a head is flipped, the pot doubles. If a tail is flipped, I get the pot and the game ends.

Thus if a tail is flipped immediately, I get $1; if a head appears and then a tail, I get $2; if two head appear and then a tail, I get $4, and so on. The problem is to determine what a fair entry fee would be—that is, the approximate amount I should pay so that I break even on the average.

Suppose you were running a casino. What's your guess as to what you would charge me so that you'd make a profit on the game? Briefly explain your guess.
