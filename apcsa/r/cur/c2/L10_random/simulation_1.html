The science of probability got started in the mid-seventeenth century in the effort to determine the likely outcomes of gambling games involving dice. You can find quite a bit of information on the Internet, but here's a quote:

Chevalier de Me´r´e [a French nobleman] gambled frequently to increase his wealth. He bet on a roll of a die that at least one 6 would appear during a total of four rolls. From past experience, he knew that he was more successful than not with this game of chance. Tired of his approach, he decided to change the game. He bet that he would get a total of 12, or a double 6, on twenty-four rolls of two dice. Soon he realized that his old approach to the game resulted in more money.

In the following BlueJ project, there is some code for generating pairs of dice rolls. The DieRollGenerator class contains methods to compute the likelihood of rolling one 6 in four rolls, testSixInFourRolls, and then a test method to simulate rolling the dice four times and seeing how often you get one 6, runTestSix. Run these two methods to discover your chance of rolling a six in four rolls and complete the methods test12In24Rolls and runTestTwelve to simulate and discover what your chances are of rolling a 12 in twenty-four rolls of a pair of dice.
