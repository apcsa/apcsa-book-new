Consider the following game, referred to as the St. Petersburg Lottery.

  1. I pay a fixed entry fee, and a pot is initialized to $1.
  2. A fair coin is flipped repeatedly. If a head is flipped, the pot doubles. If a tail is flipped, I get the pot and the game ends.

Thus if a tail is flipped immediately, I get $1; if a head appears and then a tail, I get $2; if two heads appear and then a tail, I get $4, and so on. The problem is to determine what a fair entry fee would be—that is, the approximate amount I should pay so that I break even on the average.

Suppose you were running a casino. What's your guess as to what you would charge me so that you'd make a profit on the game? Briefly explain your guess.

Suppose I pay $4 to play. If the flip is tails, I lose $3. If two flips are heads then tails, I lose $2. If three flips are heads, heads, and tails, then I break even. So it depends on how often those things happen.
I bet that charging $100 would work. There would have to be a bunch of heads in a row for the player to win.
