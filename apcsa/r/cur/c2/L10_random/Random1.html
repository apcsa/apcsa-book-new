Here's an example. Suppose the seed is 4, a is 9, b is 5, and we want a single random digit at each call (we choose the rightmost digit).

r a * r + b return value
4 9 * 4 + 5 = 41 1
1 9 * 1 + 5 = 14 4
4 9 * 4 + 5 = 41 1
...

This of course is not a very interesting random number generator, since it only returns two values, alternating between them. In a real random number generator, a and b are chosen so that the return values look randomly chosen.
