The human view of randomness differs somewhat from the statistical view. (When asked to select a "random" integer between 1 and 4, inclusive, most people will choose 3.) One of the characteristics of a good generator of random coin flips is that every possibility for a pair of flips (HH, HT, TH, and TT) should appear equally often in the sequence. The same holds for triple flips, quadruples, etc. Thus the sequence "tails tails tails" should be just as likely as the sequence "heads tails tails", even though the latter sequence looks more "random".
