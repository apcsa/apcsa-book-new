When humans are asked to choose a random number between 1 and 4 (inclusive), most of them will choose the number 3. Statistically, however, a truly random number generator would choose 1, 2, and 4 just as often as 3.

What accounts for this difference?

Numbers at the end aren't "random", and an odd number is more "random" than an even number. A real random number generator probably wouldn't think about those things.
A computer random number generator can be systematic and handle 1, 2, 3, and 4 identically. That's harder with people.
