Let's make a quick change to the Eye class by giving Albert Einstein laser beams that shoot out of his eyes. We'll do this by drawing a red line from the center of each pupil to the location of the mouse click. Save your work in a project titled XEyes_laser.

It is clear that we'll use the mouseClicked method to find out both when and where the mouse was clicked. The drawing will happen, however, in the Eye classes's draw method.

We can't pass in the click coordinates directly into the draw method, however; the draw method gets triggered by the callback method paintComponent in the XEyes class.

The right approach is the store the last mouse click location into some instance variables, and modify the draw method to use those instance variables to draw a line. Something like: 

private int clickedX;
private int clickedY;

A hint: the center of the pupil is stored in the Eyes pupilX and pupilY instance variables.
