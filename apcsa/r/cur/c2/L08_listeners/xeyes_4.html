One thing that makes the Eye class fairly daunting is all of the ... math. In order to place the pupil correctly, the Eye class uses some geometry formulas that may look even worse than when you did them in geometry class!

A lot of computer programs use mathematical modeling, which basically means using math to describe something in a real (or virtual) world. We hope, though, that if you dislike math, the neat applications that you can do with computers will make math seem cool, rather than programming seem like math. For instance:

  • Mathematical modeling is used with computers to predict weather and to think about how much our climate is likely to heat up in the coming years.
  • Mathematical modeling is used to make computer games realistic while, sometimes, still 'fantastical'. How does a building fall when a three story monster crashes into it, when you don't know what angle it will be viewed from? How should bullet trajectories and explosions look?
  • Mathematical modeling is used to change or enhance photographic images, for instance, making an image have a stained glass look, or making an image look like it was taken one hundred years ago.
