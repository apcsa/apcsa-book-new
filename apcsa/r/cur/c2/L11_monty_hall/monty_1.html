The program MontyHall models the interaction between Monty and the contestant. It consists of four classes:

  • the Monty class, whose constructor starts the program;
  • the Contestant class;
  • the Controller class, which manages the display;
  • the Stage class, which forms a part of the display.

Open the MontyHall project.

Instantiate a Monty object, then run the playGame method to play the game once. You'll be asked to click on a door, then to click on a door again (either the door you've already chosen, or a different door if you want to switch). Play additional games by calling the playGame method. In these games, try to determine from your runs whether switching is better than not switching, or vice versa.
