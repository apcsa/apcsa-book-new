You may know of the song "99 Bottles of Beer on the Wall". It goes like this:

99 bottles of beer on the wall, 99 bottles of beer.
If one of those bottles should happen to fall, 98 bottles of beer on the wall.

98 bottles of beer on the wall, 98 bottles of beer.
If one of those bottles should happen to fall, 97 bottles of beer on the wall.

Using only the Java we've seen so far, we would find it quite tedious to write a program to print those lyrics—we'd have to make many calls to System.out.println! To make the computer do something repetitively, we would call a method over and over by hand using BlueJ's Object Bench. Or in the case of an event handler like mouseClicked we would need to click the mouse multiple times to get a picture painted over and over. Upcoming activities introduce the technique of looping, enabling us to encode repetitive actions.

Examples include:

  • animation, repeating images that change slightly at each repetition to give the illusion of movement;
  • search for particular characters in a string; and
  • printing the complete lyrics of "99 Bottles of Beer on the Wall" with much less tedium.
