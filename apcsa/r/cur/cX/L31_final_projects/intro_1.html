We've got three project ideas for you, each designed to take several weeks of your dedicated class time. So, depending on how much of the school year is left, you won't be able to work on all of them.

If you are taking this course with a teacher, they will have some opinion about what you should work on and how you should proceed. If you don't have a teacher, read through the project descriptions that follow and do what you like.


  1. Adventure: This is an extension of the project and code you worked on at the end of the Inheritance unit, in lesson 18. For the final project, though, you'll get a much large chunk of time to do something interesting and creative. We have suggestions.

  2. Connect 4: You've probably played this game—two players take turns dropping pieces into a vertical game board, and the one who gets four of his or her pieces in a row wins. In this project, however, you'll make a game that can work for more than two players, can have any sized board (which changes the strategy somewhat), and lets players drop "bombs" that remove already dropped pieces (which changes the strategy a lot).

    Your big task, though, is to write a computer player that plays with some smarts. We provide quite a bit of framework code in this project.

  3. Aces Up: This is a solitaire card game which involves grouping and removing cards as you deal them out of a standard deck. There is some strategy involved, but not too much.

    We provide a Card class that can draw its instances with a nice picture, but you'll do the bulk of the work setting up, drawing, running the game.
