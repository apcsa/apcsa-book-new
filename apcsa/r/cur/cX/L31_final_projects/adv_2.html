Here are some more ideas for extending the adventure project:

  • An item can be put inside another item, and something new is created. Descriptions and usage should change, and suceeding in the game should depend on these item combinations.

  • Items and rooms can have states that change as they are interacted with.

  • Entry and exit from rooms is barred until particular items are possessed, not possessed, used, etc.

  • A player needs to "wait" (that is a command) in a room before certain things become possible: before an item is visible, before an exit is visible, etc. Descriptions should change.

  • Make a Character class by extending item. Characters are like other players: they hold conversations, they can move between rooms by themselves, they can take items from the player or drop them for the player.

  • Items have a certain weight, and a player has a maximum load that she can carry.

  • Do something interesting with the Player's name. (Ask the player for it before the game starts). Rooms can call the player by name, etc.

  • The could be a point system, and players "win" if they get enough points. Points could come by collecting "treasure" items, or solving puzzles, etc.

Your teacher may have a list of requirements for you to complete this project. A good starting point is that you make 5-10 "interesting" items and 5-10 "interesting" rooms, where interesting is something like the above list. And that you make your game fun to play!

In your project writeup, you will also need to include:

  • A paragraph in the executive summary describing the main scenario in your game, and a paragraph summarizing your new items and rooms.

  • A section describing each item and room in your game. For each, include the class name and a paragraph describing your implementation. Don't describe every detail of your code; rather, give us a high-level overview of what the item/room can do, and rationales behind the approach you took to implementing that functionality (i.e., why you did it that way).

  • One or more transcripts of your game being played that show all of the things that it can do. A transcript is a log of everything that the player sees while playing the game. Create these by playing your game and then copy-and-pasting the contents of the console window into your write-up. You might need several transcripts to show all the functionality of your items and rooms. For instance, if there are several ways that your game can end, you should include transcripts for each.
