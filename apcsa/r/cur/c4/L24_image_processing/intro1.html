An color image can be considered to be made up of many individual dots of color, arranged in a two dimensional grid. This is the same concept that was introduced in the lesson on graphics, and the dots have the same name: a pixel.



There are many ways to represent a color. We will use one of the most common, the RGB color model. As shown at the left, the colors red, green, and blue can be combined (added together) to make any other color. These are called the three color channels of an RGB image.


For our images, we'll let the amount of each color range from 0 (no color at all) to 255 (full saturation of that color). Each pixel, then, is represented by a set of three numbers, like (255,0,200), which would be a very purple dot: full red, no green at all, and quite a bit of blue.
