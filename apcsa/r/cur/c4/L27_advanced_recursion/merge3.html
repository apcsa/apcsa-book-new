Let's give names to the three arrays. The two arrays to be combined we'll call source1 and source2, and the array into which they'll be combined will be destination. Thus:

source1 source2 destination
1 4 7 9 2 3 6 8 1 2 3 4 6 7 8 9

One approach for producing the sorted result is to first search for the smallest element, then the next smallest element, and so on. Because the arrays being combined are sorted, however, we can do this more efficiently.

The algorithm uses three index variables. One, which we'll call ks1, is the position of the smallest source1 element not yet copied into destination. The second, which we'll call ks2, plays a similar role with source2. The third is the position of the next unfilled spot in destination; we'll call that kd.

The main part of the algorithm is a loop that copies the smaller of the two values source1[ks1] and source2[ks2] into destination[kd], and then increments kd and either ks1 or ks2.
