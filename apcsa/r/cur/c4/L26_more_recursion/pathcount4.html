Some consideration about what "nearer to the base cases" means suggests that it will help to consider paths that are one step shorter than paths to (n,k) and for which supplying one step extends the path to (n,k).

The problem statement says that every step in a path being counted has to go south or east. That means that the paths from (0,0) to (n,k) can be divided into two groups:

those whose last step is a move south from (n,k-1)

...and those whose last step is a move east from (n-1,k). These two groups cover all the paths from (0,0) to (n,k) without any duplication.
