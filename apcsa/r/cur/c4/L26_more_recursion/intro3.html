In diagram form, what's happening is this.

  1. Initially, we want to compute the sum in an interval that we'll draw as a horizontal line.

  2. The recursive calls split this line into two smaller lines and mid (drawn as a small circle).

  3. Each of those small lines are similarly split into two smaller lines and a circle ...

  4. until we reach the base case (an empty line).

If you just look at the circles in the figures above, you might imagine them as arranged in an upside-down tree. A recursion with more than one recursive call is called a tree recursion for that reason.
