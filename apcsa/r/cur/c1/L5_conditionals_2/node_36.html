There is a nice relationship between &&, ||, and !. Suppose b1 and b2 are boolean variables. Then

!(b1 && b2) is equal to !b1 || !b2
!(b1 || b2) is equal to !b1 && !b2

That is, negating an "and" gives an "or" with the arguments negated. Similarly, negating an "or" gives an "and" with the arguments negated.

These are called "DeMorgan's Laws", named after a 19th-century mathematician. You may wish to verify them using an example or a truth table.
