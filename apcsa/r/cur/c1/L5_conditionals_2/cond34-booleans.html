In English, we use words like "and" or "or" to combine expressions. Float, give us an example.


Ahem. I like playing video games at night or reading and charging my solar panel during the day.


As you can see, sloppy use of and/or statements can lead to ambiguous meaning. Does Float like gaming at night and reading/solar charging during the day? Or does he like gaming or reading at night, and solar charging during the day?


Ambiguity is not acceptable for a programming language like Java. We'll learn how to be very precise with "and/or" and similar expressions, lest the compiler scold us!
