The myCandyCount variable is keeping track of how much the CandyJar object holds. We've said that the constructor supplies the initial quantity of candy, theeat method reduces it, and the refill method increases it. How is this done?

A good way to think about a variable is as a box that holds only one thing. All variables have

  • a name, specified in the declaration,
  • a type, also specified in the declaration, that says what kind of thing it can hold,
  • and a value, what it's actually holding.

Double-clicking on candyJar1 produces a window that shows the myCandyCount box.

Variables can be thought of as boxes.

Parameters can also be thought of as boxes.
