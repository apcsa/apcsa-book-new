What is the value of 

 "" + ""

Try to guess before testing it out.

" " (Note, there is a 'space' in that string.)
Nope. A string with a space in it is quite different, to Java at least, than a string without anything in it. In the statement above, both of the strings that are being concatenated are empty. So, the space in this choice won't just appear out of the blue. Remember, programming is sometimes like satisfying a finicky teacher.
"" (There is no space in this one).
Right. Gluing together two completely empty strings (those without anything in them, even spaces) will result in an empty string. Remember, a string with a space in it, or anything, is different than an empty string.
"+"
No. The plus is an operator in this statement, and won't show up in the result string.
"0"
Nope! Where would the zero come from?
