Explain why evaluating

 12 + 23

and

 "12" + "23"

produced different results.

The "+" just knows how to treat things differently.
The difference in the two expresssion is the types of the elements. The '+' operator works differently when it is placed between numbers (ints) and strings. In the statement 12 + 23, both the elements are numbers, and the '+' operator will add them. In the statement "12" + "23", the elements are strings. You know this because of the double-quotation marks. When given strings, the '+' operator does a concatenation on them. That is, it glues them together.
