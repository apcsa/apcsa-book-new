So far, we've thought about how things in the real world have certain features that we will use to help us design computer programs. In the last activities, we focused on a jar of candy and the CandyJar program.

As we thought more about what features a jar of candy might have, and more about what sorts of new features a computer representation might have (e.g., averageUse), we modified our original CandyJar class. In this lesson, we are going to take this process farther, and look at very different kinds of things in the real world.

Without ruining the punchline, you will start to see how real-world objects that are obviously quite different can be represented by very similar Java classes.

This "finding what is the same" is a very important part of being a good programmer.
