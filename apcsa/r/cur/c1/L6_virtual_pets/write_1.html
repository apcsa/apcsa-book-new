One thing that makes programming fun is seeing your own ideas come to life on the computer. To do this, you often need extended time to play with your program and get it just right. And, needless to say, this is a different process than you go through when you do homework or answer multiple-choice questions.

So, you are going to take some time now to make a large and interesting virtual pet program.

Your teacher, if you are taking this course with one, may have more specific information about what is expected of you, both in the finished product and how long you are allowed to take.

On our part, we are just going to suggest some things you might get your virtual pet to do. You don't need to follow any of these in particular (unless your teacher says differently). A good-sized program, however, will incorporate dozens of different moods, necessitating a similarly large number of instance variables. And, there should be complicated interactions that can suprise the person using your program. You want them to have fun, don't you?
