Why are we using curly brackets when they're not needed? There are several reasons.

  • Consistency: If we use curly brackets all the time, we don't have to think about when to use them and when not to use them. Our brains are thus less cluttered.

  • Ease of modification: We often have to add statements in an if...else statement, for example, to produce debugging output. If we're adding a statement to an if...else body with only one statement and curly brackets not used, we have to insert a pair of curly brackets along with the new statement. Though it may not seem like a big deal, it's one extra thing to forget.

  • Error avoidance: see next step.
