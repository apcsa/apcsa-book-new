Many computer programs rely on accurate timekeeping procedures to correctly function. Sometimes, a small detail in how a computer keeps track of the date can lead to huge problems and widespread panic (like in the Y2K scare: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Y2K). One of the big issues in counting days is to make sure that leap years and leap days are accounted for. Handling this issue begins with recognizing when a given year is in fact a leap year.
