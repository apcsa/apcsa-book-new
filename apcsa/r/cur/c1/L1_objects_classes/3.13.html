Methods are "called" in Java

Methods are performed by being called. This might happen as the result of an event, say, a keystroke being typed, a mouse clicked or moved, or a button pressed. Methods may also call other methods.

A constructor method is called when an object is first created. When the method is performed—executed is the technical term—its statements are executed in the sequence they appear in the method's body, except in the following situations.

  • When a method calls another method, the called method's first statement is the next one to be executed.
  • A special kind of statement, called a conditional statement, allows statements to be selectively executed. We'll look into conditional statements later in the course.
  • Another special kind of statement, called a repetition, allows statements to be repeated. We'll also look into repetition statements later in the course.

A method may also return information to the method that called it.
