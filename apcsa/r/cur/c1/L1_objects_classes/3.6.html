An object may have other objects in its internal state.

For example, a person's state might include her age (a number), her Social Security number (another number or maybe a string), her name (maybe a string, but we'll explore this further in a bit), and so forth. The person's state might also have information about who her father is: a person object with its own name, age, and so forth.

Also consider a deck of cards, which will contain a sequence of card objects in its internal state.


We'll explore ways to build objects from an assembly of different objects (like age, SS#, name, etc.) or from an assembly of similar objects (like names, other people, and cards).
